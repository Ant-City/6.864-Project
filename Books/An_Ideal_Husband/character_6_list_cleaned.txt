


[CHARACTER] - 
      a pleasant and popular woman with "gray hair a la marquise and good lace," [CHARACTER] appears at the dinner party in act i and visits lady chiltern in ii, both times with mrs. cheveley in arm. [CHARACTER] is emblematic of an older generation of so ciety women, bemoaning the effect of politics and the higher education of women on married life. in this sense, she counterpoises the victorian new woman embodied by lady chiltern.
